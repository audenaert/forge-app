## Description

CDH involves specific activities: conducting interviews, capturing snapshots, mapping assumptions as a team, designing experiments. These activities produce the artifacts that populate the discovery graph. But if those activities happen elsewhere (a whiteboard, a Miro board, a conversation) and results are entered into the tool after the fact, the tool becomes a graveyard of stale artifacts.

The gap between "where thinking happens" and "where artifacts are stored" is where fidelity is lost. Teams forget to update the tool, or they update it days later with a summary that loses nuance. The opportunity is to collapse this gap — make the tool the surface where discovery activities happen in real time, so artifacts are created as a byproduct of the work rather than as a separate documentation step.

## Evidence

- Common failure mode with project management tools: teams stop updating Jira because the cost of keeping it current exceeds the value of having it current
- Miro/FigJam are used for live workshops because they support real-time manipulation, but the outputs aren't structured and decay immediately after the session
