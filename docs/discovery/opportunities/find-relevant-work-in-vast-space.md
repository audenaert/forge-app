## Description

STUB — to be developed.

Findability at scale is a different job from sensemaking. Here the person has a specific question — "what assumptions do we have about onboarding friction?", "which ideas did we defer last quarter for revisit?", "have we explored anything adjacent to X?" — and they need to reach the relevant pocket of the graph without walking every edge.

Browsing breaks first. Search is necessary but not sufficient — a keyword match on "onboarding" returns dozens of weakly-related artifacts when what you want is a curated slice by topic, time, owner, state, or relationship. Saved views and faceted filters start to matter. Natural-language query ("show me untested high-importance assumptions under the activation objective that haven't been touched in 30 days") starts to matter when the query language in the user's head is richer than any menu can expose.

Distinct from sensemaking (which is about building a mental model of the whole) and from navigation (which is about moving without getting lost). Findability is targeted retrieval.
