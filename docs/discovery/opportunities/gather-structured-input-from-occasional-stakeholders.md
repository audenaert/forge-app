## Description

Discovery is not a solo activity. PMs need signal from people who aren't daily users of the discovery tool — executives, adjacent-team leads, sales, support, sometimes customers directly. Those people shouldn't have to learn the whole tool to participate.

Today this gap is filled by:

- **Google Forms / Typeform surveys** — results copied back into the tool by hand and often stale by the next session.
- **1:1 DMs and Slack threads** — the PM collates responses manually, losing nuance and attribution.
- **Meeting notes** — high-fidelity in the moment, low-fidelity a week later.
- **"I'll get back to you"** — that never does.

Every hop out of the tool is a place where fidelity, structure, and traceability leak. The opportunity is a lightweight stakeholder surface — a link, an email prompt, a Slack integration — that shows a focused prompt and collects a structured response, linked back to the discovery graph where the request came from.

## Evidence

- Almost every prioritization exercise or assumption validation in the wild starts in a discovery tool, jumps out to a form or chat, and returns as stale data by hand.
- The gap between "where thinking happens" and "where artifacts are stored" (`no-place-for-discovery-activities`) is even sharper for occasional stakeholders — they have zero tool affinity and will default to whatever costs them least.
- Sales and support teams frequently say "just ask me" but the answer lives in a thread, not the graph.
