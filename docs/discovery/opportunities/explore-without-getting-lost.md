## Description

STUB — to be developed.

Exploration is non-goal-directed: the person is wandering, following interesting threads, hoping to notice something they didn't know they were looking for. That's how a lot of creative work happens. At scale the wandering breaks down in two ways:

1. **Anxiety** — "where am I, how did I get here, and what am I missing by being here instead of somewhere else?" This is navigation-safety as affect, not just affordance.
2. **Infinite branching** — every artifact has relationships in several directions; following them all is a fractal. The user needs to prune without feeling like they're closing doors permanently.

The M1 hypertext model is clean but has no orientation layer. There's no "you are here," no history of where you've been, no way to mark a thread for later without losing your place.

Distinct from findability (which is targeted) and from sensemaking (which is constructive). This opportunity is about the *state* of wandering being productive rather than paralyzing.
