## Description

STUB — to be developed.

Workshops (design sprints, opportunity brainstorms, assumption-surfacing sessions) produce *a lot* of material very fast. A one-hour ideation session with 8 people can generate 200+ raw ideas. Most of the value is in the convergence step that follows: selecting, clustering, merging duplicates, surfacing themes, deciding what gets carried forward and what gets archived for later.

This is a real-time editorial act. It's curation under time pressure, with many authors, in front of a live audience — distinct from the solo-reflective sensemaking that a PM does the week after. The material is raw, partially-formed, sometimes duplicative, sometimes contradictory.

Tools today either leave the workshop output in a sticky-note Miro graveyard (no structure, no follow-through) or force it into a clean artifact graph too early (premature formalization, loss of context). Etak sits in an interesting middle ground: the graph is structured enough to hold the output, but the convergence affordances don't yet exist.

Distinct from the other three specializations because it's synchronous, multi-author, and operating on just-generated material — the cognitive and social dynamics are different from the retrospective cases.
