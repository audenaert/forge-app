## Description

STUB — to be developed.

Sensemaking is the first casualty of scale. Once the opportunity space passes the point where a single person can hold it in mind, everyone loses their mental model — not just of the whole, but of their own corner. The symptom is the creeping feeling that you've forgotten something important, or that you're missing connections you'd see if you could just hold it all at once.

This is distinct from findability — sensemaking is constructive (building a model) rather than retrieval (finding a specific thing). It's also distinct from navigation safety (not getting lost) — sensemaking is about being *oriented*, which is a precondition for navigation at all.

Typical failure modes at scale: PMs return from vacation and can't reconstruct what they cared about last month; new team members can't see the shape of the space even when every artifact is visible to them; leadership loses confidence that anyone actually has a handle on the whole.
