## Description

As AI collapses the cost of building software, the bottleneck in product development shifts upstream — from "can we build it?" to "should we build it?" Teams that ship the most features don't win; teams that develop deep understanding of their users and creatively explore the opportunity space do.

Creative problem solving in product development is a structured cognitive process: build internal understanding of a domain, externalize that understanding into a shared model, collaborate with others to manipulate and refine that model, and rapidly pose and validate ideas as understanding evolves.

Today this process is poorly supported by tooling. Whiteboards capture spatial thinking but lose structure. Project management tools impose structure but kill creative exploration. The artifacts of discovery — opportunities, ideas, assumptions, experiments, evidence — form a rich graph of interconnected knowledge, but that graph exists only in people's heads and scattered across tools.

Forge Workspace's objective is to provide the computational substrate for this creative exploration process: a shared, structured, queryable model of the opportunity space that teams and AI agents can collaboratively build, traverse, and evolve.

## Target User

Small, product-oriented teams working in close collaboration with end-users and stakeholders. Cross-functional groups doing continuous discovery — where the developer's role is increasingly to guide agentic builders toward the right deliverables rather than write all the code themselves.

## Key Result

Teams using Forge make better product decisions — they build things that deliver value to users and support the business model — not just more things, faster.
